Evidence File 01

The Mill Runs. Nothing Gets Ground.

The 3,500 kW cement mill runs at full speed for 6 hours every day while waiting for clinker feed from the kiln — drawing 21,000 kWh of power with zero production output.
+₹9/T
Cost Impact
Mill Idle Hours vs Production Hours — Monthly (FY24)
Investigator's note: The kiln scheduling team and the mill operations team work off different planning cycles. When the kiln cuts output for maintenance, no one tells the mill to slow down. The motor keeps spinning. The meter keeps running.

Evidence File 02

The Invisible Current — Power Factor Drain

Plant-wide power factor sits at 0.82 — far below the 0.95 utility threshold. DISCOM imposes a reactive energy penalty of ₹41 Lakh/year. Every motor running below spec bleeds reactive power, unseen in daily logs.
+₹3/T
Cost Impact
Power Factor by Circuit — Current vs Target
Investigator's note: Cement mill drives, raw mill fans, and the crusher conveyor system each show a power factor below 0.80. No automatic power factor correction (APFC) panels exist at the feeder level. The plant pays a reactive energy surcharge every billing cycle — it appears as a single line on the electricity bill, never flagged.
